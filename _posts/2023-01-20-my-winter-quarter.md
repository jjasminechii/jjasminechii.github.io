# First quarter as a TA for [CSE122: Introduction to Computer Programming][work]!

I applied to become a TA for the CSE 12X series around mid November of 2022. The CSE 12X series is the Introductory to Programming courses that are offered at UW for people who are just starting to learn how to code. After a round of written applications, a teaching video screen, I received the oppurtunity to interview for the position. The interview felt quite short but it was jam-packed with information and included two different demonstration of my teaching ability and style. When I was walking out of the interview room, I honestly felt really nervous and anxious about how it went but rather than stress about it, I tried to put it out of my mind and enjoy my winter break. A few weeks later with a stroke of luck, I received the offer to become a TA for CSE 122!

One reason I was specifically interested in becoming a TA for the CSE 12X series is because these courses are newly minted. What I mean by that is what was originally 2 courses for the intro series has now become 3 courses. The reason for this? Diversity! We really want a wider range of people and perspectives coming in to learn how to code. The 3 course series takes into people from all different backgrounds and allows more flexibility in planning and time for learning. Rather than trying to cram our entire intro series into 2 courses, we've expanded it to 3 courses so that we can take more time to dive deeper into certain topics and ensure that everyone is able to understand the contents of course. Which is pretty awesome in my opinion :)
